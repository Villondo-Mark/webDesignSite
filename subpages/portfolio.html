Lakeside Ballerina Animation Project Featuring Music from Anastasia (1997)

This one was actually one of my favorite projects. This project was done to show proficiency with Adobe AfterEffects, and we were allowed to take a previous project and incorporate it into this new animation project. I immediately knew that I wanted to take this previous piece, originally a sticker, and turn it into something special. I already had it in mind before, but this opportunity came and I decided that I might as well have fun with it. The music featured is a piano cover done by piano Youtuber Kyle Landry of the song "Once Upon A December" in the move Anastasia (1997). I've always liked this song since I rediscovered both it and the movie years ago, and was originally the inspiration for the sticker in the first place. I took each visual element and animated it with the idea of a music box in mind. This project recieved a passing grade as the final project for the class that it was presented in.

Moby Dick Book Custom Book Covers Project

Moby Dick Book Cover 1 (shown above)

This was a set of pieces done for a graphic design class. One to feature typography, one to use any visual style, and another to use photography. The last one can be seen in the link to the Behance page. I wanted to create posters that reflected my idea of the book. For one, it was an old book. I decided that I would have one cover be done with grain, simple shapes a bright, contrasting colors that, to me, felt reminiscent of older book covers from the 80's. The other, featuring Typography, was done in a way that looked simple but conveyed some of the ideas of the book nonetheless. I took the idea of the titular creature, Moby Dick, an unbounded, larger than life beast that lurks below the surface of the waters, and applied it to the title with opacity and gradients to really capture those concepts. You can read the rest on Behance.

Word Poster Project feat. Baskerville MNT Pro

This was a project done in my Typography class. The goal was to create a poster featuring the unique characteristics of a chosen type, in my case, Baskerville MT Pro. When I see this type, I see it's origins and where it's been used. I imagine older books in the libraries of our schools and towns, yellowed with age, featuring this type to deliver thoughts and ideas, reflecting a sense of wisdom lost to time. With this in mind, I chose the colors that helped bring those thoughts to life. I composed it in a way that incorporated modern styles and tried to bring it into the 21st century. The stained texture was taken in Photoshop and applied in order to reflect a sense of age that often accompanied it in my thoughts. This project piece was given passing remarks upon it's turning in.
